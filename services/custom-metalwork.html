❌ Wrong Proportions Ruin the Design

Catalog Cupolas Force You Into Wrong Sizes

The Problem: Catalog cupolas come in 3-4 standard sizes. Your barn needs 42" but they only offer 36" or 48". Too small looks ridiculous. Too large overwhelms the structure. Either way, it looks wrong—and everyone can tell.

Our Solution: We design and fabricate custom cupolas sized precisely for your structure's scale. The proportions look right because they are right—calculated specifically for your building's dimensions and roof pitch.

Choose the Style That Defines Your Space

From exterior architectural elements to interior design features—custom fabrication for distinctive properties.

Cupolas & Roof Elements

Custom copper or zinc cupolas add architectural distinction to barns, garages, homes, and commercial buildings. Not catalog sizes—designed and fabricated proportionally to your structure's scale.

  • Custom sizing for perfect proportions
  • Ventilation louvers or decorative windows
  • Weather vanes and custom finials
  • Traditional or contemporary designs

Finials & Awnings

From simple spear points to elaborate custom designs, finials add vertical emphasis. Custom metal awnings provide weather protection with architectural style—standing seam, curved profiles, or contemporary planes.

  • Roof peak and cupola finials
  • Entrance and window awnings
  • Copper, zinc, or stainless steel
  • Functional + distinctive design

Interior Metalwork

Our metalworking expertise extends inside—handcrafted copper sinks, distinctive fireplace surrounds, metal countertops, and custom furnishings that become focal points of interior design.

  • Handcrafted copper sinks (kitchen, bar, bath)
  • Fireplace surrounds and hearths
  • Metal countertops and backsplashes
  • Custom tables, shelving, furnishings

Restoration & Historic Matching

Historic buildings often feature custom metalwork that's deteriorated or missing. We specialize in faithful reproductions—working from photos, fragments, or architectural drawings to recreate missing elements.

  • Historic cornice and trim reproduction
  • Matching original designs and proportions
  • Period-appropriate materials and finishes
  • Architectural preservation metalwork
